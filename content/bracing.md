## Bracing

After the soundboards were cut, the bracings were next. It ‘gave voice’ to the board, and they needed to have specific shapes.

![Screenshot 2026-03-10 at 1.01.34 AM.png](/notion-images/bracing/Screenshot_2026-03-10_at_1.01.34_AM.png)

![Screenshot 2026-03-10 at 1.57.02 AM.png](/notion-images/bracing/Screenshot_2026-03-10_at_1.57.02_AM.png)

Since the backboard had thicker braces, I was able to get pieces of wood and hand carve them. I had the long piece going down the middle and I learned how to use the sled on the table saw to get a clean groove, and not have to chisel out the material. I then chamfered down ends so they could meet the board. 

For the front, I laser cut the pieces as they were more intricate and overlapped on more faces. I was able to do the Torres Style ‘Fan Bracing’ and also added a donut ‘ring’ on the back side of the Aztec outer ring to give it some more support. I followed a bunch of different videos and websites to get the best layout, and to also get a sense of what I needed to do.
