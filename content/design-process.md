# ‘Dog Bones’

As for every project that has been ever made, the first product isn’t ever the same as the final product. As you go along, you learn new tricks, programs, or even design theories; and this is exactly how it went for this arm as well.

These 2 versions here were my first two of seven that I started off with. The typical dog bone or “link” was separated in half so the servo could be placed in and the wire could be wrapped inside following a tunnel. We later realized  this would add many more unnecessary components and we had a goal in mind for this arm, least possible components with the same number of movement. The excessive amount of filament was taken out from many places and that also cut on print time.

A simple geometric trick was used to also keep the dog bone as one piece. Pythagorean theorem. Since we knew the length of the servo at the bottom was 40mm, we needed clearance so it could slide in easier.

# Gripper

The gripper was hard work as well. Since it was at the end of the arm, we couldn’t use the regular MG996R because it would’ve been too heavy. An SG90 was used instead which provided enough torque to hold small objects. Many design considerations went into it, but since the servo was small, we did not have much to work with. Below are some designs we considered.  

![Screenshot 2024-10-07 at 4.36.43 AM.png](/notion-images/design-process/Screenshot_2024-10-07_at_4.36.43_AM.png)

![Screenshot 2024-10-07 at 4.37.45 AM.png](/notion-images/design-process/Screenshot_2024-10-07_at_4.37.45_AM.png)

![IMG_7737 2.jpg](/notion-images/design-process/IMG_7737_2.jpg)

![IMG_7738 2.jpg](/notion-images/design-process/IMG_7738_2.jpg)

The different designs had their own problems, especially when it came to attaching to the rest of the arm. The two left ones experienced weird geometry issues and the top right one was difficult to print since 3D printed gears are not the best. The bottom right was eventually used, and with some geometry changes, it was the best one.

![IMG_7733 2.jpg](/notion-images/design-process/IMG_7733_2.jpg)

![IMG_7729 2.jpg](/notion-images/design-process/IMG_7729_2.jpg)

The bases for the gripper had two orientations, a vertical and a horizontal one. Since it was weird to translate the rotational motion vertically, it was abandoned and the horizontal version was picked. We made a plate for the servo and we had this sleek design.
