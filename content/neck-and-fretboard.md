Again, to add my own flair onto the project, I wanted to add a lot of my culture as well as my own touches. Another one of the things that I did was engrave my initials onto the headstock- PAGDA- short for *Paul Alejandro Galvan De Alba.* This design came when talking to my parents, and my dad has recently discovered the power of AI. He helped come up with some designs, and some of my input I was able to get a vision for it. I was able to laser engrave the design onto the headstock with a lot of aligning and test cutting.

Apart from that, I also really enjoyed the curve that I added to the body, the venetian cut, so I decided to cut my headstock into the similar shape as well.

As for the fretboard, I hand cut every single metal fret and hammered it into place, and I tried my best to make them not sharp on the ends to not cut myself. I glued the fretboard onto the neck beforehand, as it made the fret metal piece easier.
