## Original Design

This guitar project originally started with the research of what guitars to build and the history behind them. I wanted to give homage to my heritage, and decided to go with the requinto- more known as the Spanish Guitar. This is a guitar that is used in many corridos, bandas, mariachis, and its known for its higher pitch notes, and smaller size. I found this [website](http://www.antoniodetorres.es/index.php?option=com_guitarbook&view=detail&id=26&Itemid=2) which is where I based a lot of my measurements off of. 

![Screenshot 2026-02-05 at 12.20.53 AM.png](/notion-images/onshape/Screenshot_2026-02-05_at_12.20.53_AM.png)

I started my CAD using Onshape, and made this original design. 

![Screenshot 2026-02-05 at 12.12.39 AM.png](/notion-images/onshape/Screenshot_2026-02-05_at_12.12.39_AM.png)

The one on the right was my original design and it followed more the traditional shape of a requinto without the Venetian cut. I wanted to give the guitar some flair, so I chose to do the venetian cutway. I didn’t realize then that the cutaway wasn’t for visual effects, rather than hitting the higher frets. On my design, I didn’t flush the end to the neck, and this wasn’t really an issue.
