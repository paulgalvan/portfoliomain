As Elles was being programmed and built, we realized that certain parts could be improved upon for stability.

- Changes Made
    
    ![Screenshot 2023-07-28 at 2.18.47 PM.png](/notion-images/part-1-creating-elles-instructions/Screenshot_2023-07-28_at_2.18.47_PM.png)
    
    At the base, more studs were added so it would not break as easily.
    
    ![Screenshot 2023-07-28 at 2.23.03 PM.png](/notion-images/part-1-creating-elles-instructions/Screenshot_2023-07-28_at_2.23.03_PM.png)
    
    Here, the 3 long technic pieces had to be adjusted so both pieces could be put in.
    
    ![Screenshot 2023-07-28 at 2.15.58 PM.png](/notion-images/part-1-creating-elles-instructions/Screenshot_2023-07-28_at_2.15.58_PM.png)
    
    The 2 Essential motros were replaced by two SPIKE Prime medium motors. Choosing one or the other, the stability of the legs falls with too much motion. A change couldn't be made without the whole base being rebuilt.
    
    ![Screenshot 2023-07-28 at 2.16.49 PM.png](/notion-images/part-1-creating-elles-instructions/Screenshot_2023-07-28_at_2.16.49_PM.png)
    
    On both arms, the back grey pieces weren’t strong enough to hold the torque from the arm, so it was replaced by the yellow T piece. 
    
    ![Screenshot 2023-07-28 at 2.17.22 PM.png](/notion-images/part-1-creating-elles-instructions/Screenshot_2023-07-28_at_2.17.22_PM.png)
    
    At the waist two black 1x3x3 lift-arms were added since the hips weren’t able to spin freely, thus adding support.
