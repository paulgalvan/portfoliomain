# Printing Set Up

This section will cover the print setups and orientations needed for the optimization of print time as well as **robustness** for the arm. Since all of the servos (MG996R and SG90) need to be held down, screw and nut shafts were created for them. Taking out filament (support material) from these tiny holes is very difficult, so to counteract that, the ‘tree supports’ were used. They are faster to print and easier to take off. It also does not fill the holes usually, which is what isn’t desired. 

All the parts are also spread out to show printing time as well as orientations, but they multiple things can print together if they fit on the print bed.

## Our Printer

![bambu-lab-x-carbon-cambo.jpg](/notion-images/printing/bambu-lab-x-carbon-cambo.jpg)

The printer that we used on our 3D prints was a Bambu Carbon X1C. The Smooth PEI Plate/High Temp Plate was used to give a smooth shell to every component of the arm. Our color used was Bambu PLA Metal.
