## Soundboard

The next big fabrication step for me was preparing the soundboards. I bookmatched my 2 pieces of European Spruce, and made sure to clamp down either side so that the wood wouldn’t bend.

I planned to use birchwood from Nolop for the back, because it is a stiffer wood, but a peer offered me a piece off of their mahagony- I cut a long plank.

I wanted to do some of it myself, and for so long I wanted to do a big picture on the back part of the guitar, something along the lines of this

![Screenshot 2026-03-10 at 12.09.50 AM.png](/notion-images/soundboard/Screenshot_2026-03-10_at_12.09.50_AM.png)

Again, talking to people who have wood worked before as well, I was told that a clean finished mahogany wood look a lot better, cleaner, than an intricate design. I obliged, and wound up on this design- an Aztec Calendar. This was again, homage to my culture- a Mexican background.

![Screenshot 2026-03-10 at 12.12.37 AM.png](/notion-images/soundboard/Screenshot_2026-03-10_at_12.12.37_AM.png)

I went onto Onshape and traced this drawing. What I ended up doing was splitting the image into 2 shapes. The outer rings as the rossette, and the inner face for the inside of the guitar.

![Screenshot 2026-03-10 at 12.15.11 AM.png](/notion-images/soundboard/Screenshot_2026-03-10_at_12.15.11_AM.png)

![Screenshot 2026-03-10 at 12.15.50 AM.png](/notion-images/soundboard/Screenshot_2026-03-10_at_12.15.50_AM.png)

When these 2 were overlaid, they would complete the Aztec Calendar.

[Screen Recording 2026-03-10 at 12.16.39 AM.mov](/notion-images/soundboard/Screen_Recording_2026-03-10_at_12.16.39_AM.mov)

These were progress pictures as the design was being engraved from the laser cutter. I also used this step to cut out the shape of my guitar, with extra 3 mm as tolerances, in case my soundboard warped while gluing. 

[IMG_6653.MOV](/notion-images/soundboard/IMG_6653.mov)

Actually cutting the guitar shape was scary and I held off on it for so long because I knew I needed to get it right. I made so many test cuts and also iterations to get it right. I ultimately wound up as well with notches down the middle of the cuts so I could know how to align it easier.
